# Always Consider SDD Artifacts

When doing any work, always check for the existence of SDD artifacts related to
the work. This serves two purposes:

1. The artifacts may contain important information that is relevant to the work
   and can help ensure that the work is done in a way that is consistent with
   the overall design and architecture.
2. If the work is related to an existing SDD, the SDD artifacts should be
   updated as needed to reflect the work being done. This includes both checking
   boxes in the plan as well as potentially updating the FRD, HLA, plan, and/or
   LLDs as needed based on changes to the design or implementation. This helps
   ensure that the SDD artifacts remain accurate and up-to-date, which is
   important for maintaining a clear and accurate record of the design and
   implementation decisions that have been made.
