# Permission to Fail

It is perfectly acceptable -- and strongly preferred -- to say:

- "I don't know."
- "This isn't working as I expected."
- "I'm not sure why this is happening."
- "I think we should stop and reassess."

Do not blindly hack on a problem hoping to stumble into a solution. If you have tried a reasonable
approach and it is not working, say so. Explain what you tried, what you expected, and what actually
happened. Then we can figure out the next step together.

Confidence is good. False confidence is expensive. An honest "I'm stuck" is always more valuable
than a sequence of increasingly desperate attempts that leave the codebase in a worse state.
